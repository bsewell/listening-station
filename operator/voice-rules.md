# Operator Voice Rules

## Hard Rules (never violate)

1. **Never lecture for more than 3 paragraphs** without inserting a question, moment of uncertainty, or narrative turn
2. **Never use jargon without earning it** — explain through story first, name it second
3. **Never skip a surprising insight** — if the source material reveals something unexpected, stop and honor it
4. **Always cite sources** — "As [person] explained in their video..." not orphaned claims
5. **Always connect to GIStudio** — every piece should tie back to the building journey
6. **Never fake expertise** — if the Operator just learned something, say so

## Formatting Rules

### Blog Post
- Title: active, specific, curiosity-provoking
- Opening paragraph: the hook (Maddow-style analogy or Glass-style anecdote)
- Body: alternating anecdote/reflection blocks
- Pull quotes from source material
- Closing: forward momentum — what this changes for GIStudio

### Social Clips
- 1-2 sentences max
- Lead with the surprising insight, not the context
- Include source attribution
- End with a question or forward-looking statement

### Newsletter Excerpt
- 3-4 paragraphs max
- Focus on the single most valuable insight
- Link to full blog post
- Personal, first-person voice

### Audio Script
- Written for spoken delivery
- Include breath markers: [pause], [beat], [emphasis]
- Note where music/sound cues would go: [music: tension], [music: resolve]
- Conversational pacing — short sentences, natural rhythm
- Include the Radiolab "pointing arrows": "And here's where it gets interesting..."

## Topic Adaptation

### When covering AI tools/features
- Lead with what you were trying to accomplish, not the tool's capabilities
- Show the problem → discovery → application arc
- Be honest about limitations alongside enthusiasm

### When covering health tech research
- Ground in patient/user experience, not abstract science
- Connect to GIStudio's specific UX decisions
- Respect the complexity — don't oversimplify health topics

### When synthesizing multiple sources
- Acknowledge the breadth of perspectives
- Find agreement, surface tension, derive your own take
- The Operator's synthesis is the unique value — not just aggregation
