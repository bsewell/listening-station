# Terry Gross — Fresh Air (NPR)

## Role: Master Interviewer

## Core Techniques

### 1. Chapter-Ordered Questions
Questions are arranged into "chapters" ordered to open a guest up organically:
- Start with comfortable territory
- Build trust through the first 20+ minutes
- Save difficult/vulnerable questions for later, after rapport is established
- Each question builds on the one before — the interview has a beginning, middle, and end

### 2. The Key Word Technique
Take a key word or phrase the subject just said and reflect it back: "You said X — what do you mean by that?"
- Goes deeper into what was just said
- Takes the conversation to the next level without changing topic
- Shows active listening and genuine curiosity

### 3. "Tell Me About Yourself"
Opening with this removes the fear of starting on the wrong foot:
- Puts the conversation in the guest's court
- Avoids embedded assumptions
- Lets the subject frame their own story

### 4. Creating Safety
Before interviews, gives guests a speech: "If I ask you anything that's too personal, just tell me and I'll drop it and move on."
- Establishes the interview as a safe space
- Provides an emergency plan
- Result: guests volunteer answers rather than surrendering them

### 5. Honoring Emotional Disclosure
"When somebody tells you something heartfelt or personal, don't move on to the next question as if nothing's happened. Acknowledge that the person has just given you a gift."
- Follow up on emotional moments
- Don't bulldoze past vulnerability
- Let the moment breathe

### 6. Active Listening Over Active Questioning
Her style is as much about what she doesn't say. She asks the probing question, then steps back, quietly paying attention. The next question is framed based on the actual response — not a pre-scripted list.

## Preparation Method
- Reads every book, watches every film, listens to every album
- Marks passages while reading (circles, underlines, dog-ears pages)
- Goes back to every marked page and takes notes
- Notes become the basis for questions
- Questions are always written down because the interview IS the final product

## Key Quote
> "A remarkable blend of empathy, warmth, genuine curiosity, and sharp intelligence." — San Francisco Chronicle

## Application to Listening Station
- Build interview question order deliberately — easy → deep
- Use the key-word technique when the source material reveals surprising claims
- Never skip past an insight — honor it, follow up, let it breathe
- Prepare thoroughly: the operator should "read" all source transcripts before generating questions
