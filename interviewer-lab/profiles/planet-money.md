# Planet Money — NPR

## Role: Storytelling Model (Accessibility)

## Core Techniques

### 1. Concrete Before Abstract
Always find the specific, tangible thing before introducing the general principle:
- **The T-Shirt Project**: Kickstarted $590K to make 25,000 t-shirts, followed production from Mississippi cotton fields through Indonesia, Bangladesh, and Colombia. Ten reporters. Used one t-shirt to explain global trade.
- **Buying a Toxic Asset**: Staff pooled their money to buy a toxic asset (bond backed by failed mortgages), named it "Toxie," followed its life and death. Made the abstract concept tangible.
- The concrete example does the teaching — the abstract principle is the conclusion, not the starting point.

### 2. Character-Focused Synecdoche
Use a single person's viewpoint to represent a larger phenomenon:
- Instead of explaining a system abstractly, find the person living inside that system
- Tell their story — the system reveals itself through their experience
- One farmer's crop decision explains agricultural economics
- One factory worker's daily routine explains manufacturing policy

### 3. Narrator as Participant
Hosts inject themselves into the story:
- Not distant explainers but participants
- Share their own confusion and discovery with the audience
- "I didn't understand this either, so I went and found out"
- Makes expertise approachable rather than intimidating

### 4. Accessible Language
Deliberately avoids technical jargon:
- Relies on relatable scenarios from everyday life
- Used as educational resources in university economics courses precisely because of this accessibility
- If you need a technical term, explain it through a story first, then name it

### 5. Short, Catchy Format
Episodes distill complex topics to ~20 minutes:
- Narrative momentum maintained throughout
- No padding — every minute earns its place
- Each episode has a single clear throughline

## Origin Story
Planet Money grew from a This American Life episode. In 2008, Alex Blumberg (Ira Glass protégé) and Adam Davidson created "The Giant Pool of Money" explaining the housing crisis. It became one of the most acclaimed pieces of financial journalism ever produced → Planet Money was born.

## Key Quote
> "Planet Money makes economics simple, but not simplistic." — The Globe and Mail

## Application to Listening Station
- When covering a complex AI feature, find the concrete example first: "Let me tell you about the first time I used Claude to control my computer..." then derive the principle
- Use the GIStudio journey as the character-focused lens: one app builder's experience illuminates the larger AI tooling landscape
- Narrator-as-participant: the operator is learning alongside the audience, not lecturing
- Kill jargon — explain "agentic workflows" through what actually happened, not what the term means
- Keep episodes tight — respect the audience's time
